Introduction

TuneFork is a music streaming and content creation platform, centered around letting users discover and enjoy the vast music industry. Moreover, we observe TuneFork's rapid growth within the software development space. One of our goals for TuneFork is tapping into the potential of both industries such that our products and services may improve at the rate in which the music industry evolves. Carefully building a strong foundational system to improve our process rewards the initial business investment and increases our economic utility. Check out our Playbook , which serves as a process definition guide for the development of TuneFork. More importantly, our playbook highlights the reasoning behind each step of the process:

The TuneFork Playbook

Problem Domain

Determining the scope of our problem case is important to outline. The goal is to define a process that fully covers all problem domain regions. This provides a more complete definition and ultimately drives faster production of more optimal process solutions. So, to re-iterate:

  • Our project teams are currently developing a new music streaming and content creation platform, TuneFork.
  • The platform can be divided into a client, ad generator, creation and upload tools, to name a few feature areas.
  • The organization is growing rapidly, from 5 engineers to 10 by months end, then 30 by the following month, likely 100 by the end of the quarter.
  • Designing a process playbook will help quickly train and place newly hired engineers more effectively.
  • Detailing security standards lowers rate of issues within risk management.
  • No system for process improvement scales back the rate of growth, and renders performance analysis to be unhelpful.
  • Organization growth means more opportunities to collect data and generate meaningful metrics to monitor the process health over time.
  • Sales and marketing are receiving lots of interest and have many features they want to add to the product.
  • There is a great deal of technical features that still need to be worked and show no sign of slowing.
